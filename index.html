The Albatross

Introduction

The Albatross is a large seabird known for its long wingspan and remarkable flying abilities. It can soar for hours without flapping its wings, using wind currents to stay aloft.

Key Facts

  • Albatrosses can live for up to 60 years.
  • They are found in the Southern Ocean and the North Pacific.
  • Albatrosses have the longest wingspan of any bird, measuring up to 12 feet (3.6 meters).
  • They are known for their migratory behavior, traveling vast distances across the oceans.

Conservation Status

Many species of albatross are currently at risk due to habitat destruction, climate change, and bycatch from commercial fishing. Efforts are being made to protect these incredible birds.
